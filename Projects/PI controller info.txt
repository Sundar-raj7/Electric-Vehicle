Design of closed loop buck converter using PI controller

A PI controller works by adjusting a system's output based on the current error (proportional term, Kp) and the accumulated error over time (integral term, Ki) to achieve a desired setpoint. 

For a buck converter, Kp controls the speed of response to changes, while Ki eliminates steady-state error, but their values must be carefully selected to ensure stability.

Proportional Term (Kp): This component produces an output proportional to the current error
A higher Kp value leads to a faster response, but can also cause instability or overshoot. 

Integral Term (Ki): This component accumulates the error over time and produces an output proportional to this accumulated error.
The Ki term is responsible for eliminating steady-state error, which is the difference between the desired and actual output after the system has settled. 
A higher Ki can reduce settling time but can also increase overshoot and introduce oscillations. 

Combined Output: The total control output is the sum of the proportional and integral terms, which is then used to adjust the duty cycle of the buck converter's PWM switch, thereby controlling the output voltage.
